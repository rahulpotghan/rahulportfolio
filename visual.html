SunBurst Visualization

Data Mining & Predictive Analytics

About the project

Businesses can upload data on all the items they buy and where their suppliers are located, and algorithm will generate a dashboard, explaining who the riskiest suppliers are. Then, it’s up to the businesses to seek out bad actors and reassess their suppliers. “Visualization helpes them identify where the hotspots are going to be.

My role

I started by collecting data on where red flags is most prevalent and what products it affects. Then combined that with United Nations trade data on which countries source which products from which places. And, finally, I broke down a list of thousands of business products and services into their component parts, to determine all the raw materials that are required to, say, make a computer or a telephone with help of data and market researcher. Using those three data sources, I built an algorithm that could predict how likely it was that a given product, sourced from a given place, and have red flags. The idea is that the user would contact his supplier: and tell more about where you’re getting the following parts of your computers.

Why Sunburst

The sunburst chart is ideal for displaying hierarchical data. Each level of the hierarchy is represented by one ring or circle with the innermost circle as the top of the hierarchy. A sunburst chart without any hierarchical data (one level of categories), looks similar to a doughnut chart. However, a sunburst chart with multiple levels of categories shows how the outer rings relate to the inner rings. The sunburst chart is most effective at showing how one ring is broken into its contributing pieces, while another type of hierarchical chart, the treemap chart, is ideal for comparing relative sizes.
